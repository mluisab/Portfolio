The IS&P Process In Detail

The Information Security and Privacy (IS&P) process is a risk analysis compulsory for all new projects, large-scale changes, and processing activities. The IS&P ensures that software used within the institution does not pose any risk of violating the Information Security and Privacy Statement of the UvA or the GDPR.

Why complete the process? / Reasons and Risks

Risks of not conducting an IS&P Process

An IS&P analysis serves to get a clear understanding of the privacy risks and helps to mitigate them. Therefore, failure to complete the process would pose a risk not only to you as a researcher, but also the institution as a whole. Data leaks can have serious consequences for both individuals and the UvA. For individuals, data leaks can lead to identity theft, fraud, and financial loss. For the UvA, data leaks can damage the university's reputation and cause financial loss, loss of patents, loss of sensitive research, and disrupt internal systems. Furthermore, it is mandated by law. This means that the use of non-UvA mandated software (software without an IS&P) is in breach of government/university regulation.




Benefits of conducting an IS&P

By filling in the IS&P you not only comply with the university’s regulation, but you also get a clear understanding of the privacy risks related to the use of the specific software/product. In case the software provider gets hacked, or a vulnerability is present in the software, the IS&P process makes it possible to identify the consequences and act accordingly. This helps protect students and employees against data leakage that can lead to financial loss, disruption of systems and loss of sensitive data.
