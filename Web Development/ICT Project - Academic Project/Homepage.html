Information Security & Privacy

An Information Security and Privacy (IS&P) risk assessment is a process that consists of identifying and evaluating risks associated with an organization’s information assets, such as data, systems, networks, and applications. In the case of UvA and HvA, the aim of the process is to determine the level of information security quickly and efficiently, as well as to decide whether processing poses a high risk for the rights and freedoms of data subjects. The risk assessment is currently conducted in the form of a workshop that lasts around one hour. During the workshop, the IS risk profile of the system in review is determined (Low, Moderate or High), as well as the IS and privacy measures required to ensure the system’s safe usage.



Risks

The UvA processes personal data according to the principles of the GDPR. One of these principles is Integrity and confidentiality, stating that personal data must be processed in such a manner that appropriate security is safeguarded. The IS&P risk assessment is one of the ways in which the UvA ensures the safety of the data being processed. Completing an IS&P risk assessment is compulsory for all new projects, large-scale changes and processing activities. There are significant risks associated with the non-completion of the risk assessment for the above-mentioned situations. The main and most dangerous one is a breach of security. Loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data could have severe financial, educational and reputational implications for the UvA.
